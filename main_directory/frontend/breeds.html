Top Pig Breeds for Farming

Discover the best pig breeds for your farming operation—each selected for superior genetics, productivity, and profitability.

Understanding Breed Performance

The performance of a breed depends on its genetics and the production system (intensive vs. free-range). We offer genetically superior breeds optimized for high growth rates, feed efficiency, and meat quality.

Popular Breeds and Their Statistics

🐷 Large White

Characteristics: White skin, erect ears, long body

Key Advantages: Reaches market weight (90–100kg) in 6 months; superior mothering with 10–14 piglets per litter. High productivity makes it ideal for commercial farms.

🐷 Landrace

Characteristics: Long-bodied, white skin, large drooping ears

Key Advantages: Known for high-quality lean meat and large litters (avg. 12 piglets); excellent for crossbreeding to produce hybrid gilts.

🐷 Duroc

Characteristics: Distinctive reddish-brown hair, drooping ears

Key Advantages: Rapid growth rate and muscular build; excellent feed conversion and juicy flesh ideal for light pork and heavy carcasses.

🐷 Hampshire

Characteristics: Black body with a white belt across the shoulders

Key Advantages: Regarded as a top terminal sire breed because it produces the leanest meat with minimal backfat.

🐷 Camborough

Characteristics: Hybrid crossbreed

Key Advantages: Developed for high survival rates and enhanced disease resistance in local conditions. Ideal for farmers seeking hardiness and productivity.

Why Choose Quality Breeds?

  • Faster growth to market weight (6 months avg.)
  • Larger litter sizes (10–14 piglets per sow)
  • Superior meat quality and yield
  • Better feed conversion efficiency
  • Enhanced disease resistance
  • Proven genetics with documented performance records
